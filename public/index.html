About Sustainathon

It aims to bring students with various skillsets together and to encourage them to embrace their talent in coding by participating and exploring new challenges. Students will have a wide range of opportunities to showcase their skills in problematic sectors such as education, health care, environment, and various others that need innovative solutions in today’s scenario.

The COVID-19 pandemic has proved to be a huge challenge that governments, healthcare systems, businesses, industries, and educational institutes around the world are trying to overcome. The situation is grim, but we have to make the best out of it.

The lockdown, online classes, and submissions may have made people anxious, stressed, and bored. But this event aims to drive some excitement and ambition in every individual who plans to be a part of it. This hackathon will be an opportunity for developers to make a social impact driven innovative software solution to some real problems posed by the COVID-19 epidemic.

Judging criteria

A Google Form will be provided to the contestants where they are supposed to upload their proposal’s ppt/pdf/video drive link.

The proposal being submitted should clearly mention the team name and the team leader’s name


The proposals will be judged on the following basis:

  • IDEA BEHIND THE PROJECT

    Innovation, effectiveness, and the relevance of the main idea behind the project, and the inspiration behind selecting the project will be taken into account.

  • TECHNOLOGY STACK

    What sort of technology services or set of tools were used to build/run the application? It influences the ability of your project and will be one of the deciding factors for the judges.

  • MOTIVATION

    The main idea behind the project that drives you and its originality. The motivation, where it came from, and how it was implemented.

  • HOW IT CATERS TO THE THEME

    The relatability of the idea or project to the theme assigned to you will be taken into consideration.

Rules

Each team has to adhere to the following rules in order to pitch and present your ideas in the given frame of time

  1. A valid team must comprise of 2-4 team members.

  2. A change in a team is allowed if informed beforehand. You may contact us for any changes.

  3. At the time of RSVP, the participants or the team members must provide a valid college ID to verify their details.

  4. The last date for the registration for the event is 31st October 2020.

  5. The presentation of the idea will be on zoom premium and the team will have to present and demonstrate their idea to the judges.

  6. The video of all the team members in a team shall be on during the presentation and pitch of the idea.

  7. The teams can also be made cross college.

  8. All the design elements, code, etc. for your project must be created during the event. Third-party tools are allowed. While you may not begin the coding in advance, you can plan and discuss it with your team in advance.

  9. Adding new features to an already existing project is allowed. Judges will only consider the new functionality added during the hackathon.

  10. Written documents and design sketches are allowed. You can also incorporate the material that is freely available to the public on the internet in your project. For Ex. Heroku, Netlify, etc

  11. Any intellectual property that is developed during the hackathon will belong to the team that developed it.

  12. The communication and announcements related to the hackathon will be put out on the WhatsApp group which will include all the team leaders

  13. The duration of the hackathon is 24 hours. i.e. on 5th and 6th November from 12:00 PM – 12:00 PM(IST).

Themes

NO POVERTY

To end poverty in all its forms, everywhere. It aims to eliminate all aspects of extreme poverty, like food scarcity, unhygienic drinking water and improper sanitation. It focuses on people living in poverty and also on social policies that can reduce poverty.

ZERO HUNGER

End hunger, achieve food security and improved nutrition and promote sustainable agriculture. It highlights the diverse interconnections between food security, nutrition, rural transformation, and sustainable agriculture.

GOOD HEALTH

To ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages. The goal is to achieve universal health coverage, which seeks equal access for both men and women to health services. It proposes to put an end to child mortality and epidemics.

EDUCATION

Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. The goal is to provide quality and easily accessible education for children and young people, plus other learning opportunities. Achieving universal literacy and numeracy is one of its objectives.

GENDER EQUALITY

Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls. This is to promote gender equality and encourage all women and girls. It seeks to offer equal rights to women and children, opportunities for free-living without any discrimination or violence.

CLEAN ENERGY

Ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable and modern energy for all. Access to energy is a very significant pillar for people's well-being, sustainable growth and the alleviation of poverty.

ECONOMIC GROWTH

Foster sustained, inclusive and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment and decent work for all. The goal is to achieve higher levels of economic efficiency through diversification, technical upgrading, and innovation..

INDUSTRY

Build resilient infrastructure, promote inclusive and sustainable industrialization, and foster innovation. It plays a key role in the introduction and promotion of emerging technology, in the facilitation of international trade and in the effective use of resources.

REDUCED INEQUALITY

Reduce inequality within and among countries. The SDG requires discrimination to be minimized based on income, gender, disability, race, ethnicity, heritage, religion or economic status.

SUSTAINABLE CITIES

Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient, and sustainable. It offers affordable accommodation, sustainable and efficient transportation and decreases the environmental effect on cities by using green technology.

SUSTAINABLE MANAGEMENT

To ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns. This is intended to ensure that resources are used properly, energy conservation and sustainable infrastructure are improved, access to basic services and a better quality of life for everyone is assured.

CLIMATE ACTION

Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. The main aim is to strengthen resilience and adaptive capability to climate-related disasters and the incorporation of climate change interventions into policy and planning.

BRAINSTORM

The SDGs ensure the development of all sectors of life and the environment. Some of the goals are distinct global agendas, while some can be interlinked to one another. If the project is related to more than one purpose, it would come under this category.

FAQs

A hackathon is an event, usually hosted by a tech company or organization, where programmers get together for a short period of time to collaborate on a project. The participants work rapidly and often work without sleep to achieve their task, as the events generally last 24 hours or take place over a weekend.

The central theme of the hackathon revolves around the ’17 Sustainable Development Goals’ as mentioned in the UN Resolution, called the 2030 Agenda.
